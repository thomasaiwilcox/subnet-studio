# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Subnet Studio a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness;
- respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences;
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback;
- accepting responsibility, apologising to those affected by mistakes, and learning from the experience;
- focusing on what is best for the community and its learners.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- sexualised language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces and when an individual officially represents the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported privately using the contact method on the project maintainer's GitHub profile. Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with respect for the reporter's privacy and safety.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions determined by other project leadership.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will use the following community-impact guidelines:

1. **Correction:** A private written warning for inappropriate language or unprofessional behaviour.
2. **Warning:** A warning with consequences for continued behaviour and a temporary restriction on interaction.
3. **Temporary ban:** A temporary ban from public or private project interaction for a sustained or serious breach.
4. **Permanent ban:** Permanent removal for a pattern of violations, harassment, aggression, or disparagement of protected groups.
